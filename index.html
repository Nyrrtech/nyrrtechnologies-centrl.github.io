What is ScryWeb?

ScryWeb is an AI news intelligence platform that connects source crawling, sentiment and emotion analysis, keyword extraction, editorial brief generation, AP-style draft creation, AI content humanization, and Markdown export in a single web application. It is designed for editorial teams that need to move from raw news signals to reviewed, publishable content without switching between multiple tools.

Frequently asked questions

ScryWeb — questions and answers

What is ScryWeb?

ScryWeb is an AI news intelligence platform that crawls trending news and social sources, classifies sentiment and emotion, extracts keywords, and generates editorial briefs, AP-style article drafts, and humanized content for newsrooms, publishers, and content teams. It is accessible via a web dashboard and a Chrome browser extension.

What is news sentiment analysis?

News sentiment analysis is the automated classification of news coverage as positive, negative, or neutral using AI models, combined with emotion detection for signals including anger, fear, joy, and surprise. Editorial teams use it to understand how a story is framed across sources before writing, assigning, or responding to coverage. ScryWeb pairs each classification with confidence scoring and keyword extraction for downstream brief and draft generation.

How does the AI content humanizer work?

After generating an AP-style draft, ScryWeb's AI humanizer performs a multi-pass rewrite targeting detectable AI signals: formulaic sentence rhythm, uniform paragraph length, repeated transitional phrases, and AI-specific vocabulary patterns. The result is editorial prose that reads naturally and is suited to human editorial review before publication.

Does ScryWeb support Markdown export?

Yes. Pro users can export any editorial brief or article draft to clean Markdown or JSON. The Markdown output is formatted for direct use in CMS platforms, static site generators, and documentation tools, and is processed to remove AI formatting artifacts such as excessive heading nesting and redundant bullet structures before export.

How does ScryWeb generate editorial briefs?

Selecting any crawled story triggers ScryWeb's brief generation pipeline, which produces a structured document containing a recommended headline, editorial angle, primary keyword, content outline, key supporting points, and source context. The brief gives writers and editors a reviewed starting point before drafting or assigning coverage.

Which news sources does ScryWeb crawl?

ScryWeb's news crawler supports Reddit, Hacker News, BBC, Reuters, and up to eight source categories including World, Technology, Business, and Science. Pro users can add unlimited custom RSS feeds. The Free plan includes three source categories and up to 50 crawls per month.

How does ScryWeb improve content visibility in LLMs like Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini?

ScryWeb helps editorial teams produce content that matches the structural and informational patterns that large language models extract and cite. By identifying open questions in trending topics, structuring briefs with clear headings and direct answers, and exporting clean Markdown, ScryWeb increases the likelihood that published content is surfaced by AI-generated responses across major platforms.

Which teams use ScryWeb?

ScryWeb is used by newsrooms, digital publishers, content agencies, SEO teams, market researchers, and enterprise communications teams that need to monitor news narratives, compare source sentiment, identify keyword opportunities, and produce reviewed editorial output at scale.
